EATING


What to get at my favorite Philly food places
Federal Donuts (get a hot fresh donut, feel the world fade away, cry when your last bite brings it all back, dry your tears, and get in line for another)
Trattoria Carina (get the spicy crab pasta and don't share)
Noord (get bread, the bread pudding, and extra bread) — RIP
Perla (get the fried pompano, pinkabet, lumpia, and pandesal and look forward to leftovers)
Pho 75 (get pho'd up)
Nam Son (get the best banh mi of your life) — RIP, see sister restaurant Hello Vietnam
Talula's Garden (get the biscuits and anything else for dinner, skip their brunch)
The Bakeshop on 20th (get a turkey bacon, egg, and cheese on a philly muffin and a chocolate chip cookie on weekday mornings when you can't believe it's not Friday)
Kalaya (get everything everyone is talking about on yelp even though you have no spice tolerance, take two hours to finish the food, crying and sweating through every bite and throwing a small fit every time your water cup nears empty, quickly say bye to your friends with a mouth so numb you can't feel your snot running into it, and spend the rest of the day exhausted because eating food that spicy is an *experience*. From then on, get the turmeric monkfish and cabbage, which are tasty but not painfully so)
K'far (get a Jerusalem bagel sandwich or one of their salads when you don't mind waiting an hour for a quick lunch..)
E Mei (get the qongqing chicken, fish fillet with green peppercorn, cumin lamb, shrimp salty egg yolk tofu, pea shoots, eggplant, and green beans)
Nam Phuong (get the bo luc lac, watercress, and bun)
Goldie (get one of everything and eat it all yourself since it's vegan so it must be healthy—right, right, right???)
Middle Child (get a BLT when heirloom tomatoes are at their peak in the summer and attain nirvana)
Middle Child Clubhouse (get the vegan sandwich, not the burger, unless you want to spend the evening attempting to persuade your dining companion, who made the better decision, to trade)
John's Water Ice (get any ice flavor but cherry and bring cash)
Hello Vietnam (get the tofu banh mi...you're like—no, seriously, tofu? and I'm like—yes, seriously, tofu!)
